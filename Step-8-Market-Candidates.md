Step 8:  Market Candidates

Intro
The best recruiters are usually the most proactive.

In this next clip, we’ll be explaining a proven strategy that will help you to make at least one extra placement per month.

A word of warning though.

The one extra placement per month will not happen to reactive recruiters.

Let’s take a look

Usual Pause
Whenever you interview a high-demand candidate ask bullets these types of questions

Important interview questions

Key achievements?
Which companies would you like to work for?
What can you do for those companies?
Who, if anyone do you know in those organisations?

When you call a prospect client with a high demand candidate who has strong key achievements, it’s harder for that person to resist your call.

Think about that for a moment.
High demand candidates are harder to find and it’s therefore likely that your prospect client will also struggle when they want to hire this kind of person.

Let’s rejoin Mike as he shares a story of a £250,000 (two hundred and fifty thousand pounds) fee

Outro
Mike made a very important point at the end of that short clip.

Negative recruiters tend to think that even if they were to proactively approach a target client with a high demand candidate, it’s unlikely that the employer will be hiring for someone of that profile.

Those recruiters are totally missing the point.

It’s not unknown for employers to create roles for high demand candidates. Okay, it’s not an everyday occurrence, but it happens.

As importantly, marketing candidates is a warmer sales call for you. 
And because you are focusing on a pain point of your target client– i.e. high demand candidates are harder to recruit – clients are usually more receptive to your call.

That, in itself, can result in them giving you a different vacancy. Or it could lead to you closing for a business development meeting where you can take business from your competitors.

Make no mistake, though. From time to time you will make a placement as a direct result of introducing your high demand candidate to target companies.
